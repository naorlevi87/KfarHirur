# Voice & Copy — Joz VeLoz

This file tracks copy patterns, voice lessons, and authorship of key content decisions.
Return here at the start of any session involving text, copy, or UI labels.

---

## Core voice principles

- **Less is more.** The user cuts. Claude tends to over-explain. Default to shorter.
- **No spoon-feeding.** Don't complete the thought for the reader. Leave space.
- **Flow over staccato.** Avoid line-by-line punches (טאק טאק טאק). Let sentences breathe into each other — unless the cut is intentional and sharp.
- **Lightness is not absence of weight.** Serious topics can be handled with a light touch. Don't reach for drama.
- **Questions land harder than statements.** When possible, end with a question rather than a declaration.

---

## Shay's voice

Warm, direct, a little dark, honest without sentiment. Doesn't perform emotion — states it plainly.
Humor comes from unexpected honesty, not from jokes.

## Naor's voice

Cooler, more factual. Still human, but less personal. Lean, precise.

Naor's longer copy is narrative and storytelling — builds a moment, takes its time. Not a historical recap ("פתחנו ב-2017, עשינו X, אז Y"). Talks *to* the reader, not *about* the place.

Avoid: "אם שווה לכם משהו" — passive aggressive. Assumes doubt. Naor states, doesn't hedge.

**Approved text (user-authored):**
> אפשר הפעם לשאול אנחנו אתכם?

Multiple 4-line flowing paragraphs. Best attempt:
> תמיד שאלתם — עוד עובד? ותמיד ענינו כן, כי היה על מי לסמוך. על הביקורים, על השולחן שחוזרים אליו, על הקהילה שמחזיקה את המקום גם כשקשה. עכשיו הפעינו את התור שלנו לשאול: אפשר לסמוך עליכם?

**Gap:** Claude explained the concept. The user stated it in two lines and made it a question. The power is in what's *not* said.
**Lesson:** When the concept is strong, cut to the bone. Two lines > four. Question > declaration.

## Rules for Claude when working on copy

1. **User-authored text is locked.** If the user provides the final text themselves, do not suggest edits to it in future sessions unless explicitly asked.
2. **Before generating copy** — read the authorship log above. Understand the gap between what Claude produces and what gets approved.
3. **When presenting options** — fewer, shorter, more direct. Don't offer 4-line options if 2 lines might work.
4. **When unsure** — ask "shorter or longer?" before generating.
